Field Data Collection and Analysis

We examined tree species’ functional traits including origin, seed dispersal, pollination, abundance, and diversity and the degree of litter along the path.

Methodology

Systematic sampling measured tree characteristics and litter along the path.

We selected 32 sites at 200m intervals along the path, alternating right and left, starting on the right at North Fort Street Park and ending at Old Murrayfield Railway Station. Sites were 5m long and 1m deep. Depth was measured from the back of the tree closest to the path. Trees more than 5m from the path were not sampled. We employed PictureThis mobile application to identify tree species.

We used Defra’s Litter Grading System to analyse litter quantity along the path.

Results

Using statistical analysis, we found a moderate positive correlation between environmental attractiveness and social deprivation along NLCRP, with higher deprivation associated with lower environmental attractiveness.
